BMR

BMR stands for your basal metabolic rate. In simple terms, it means how much energy your body would burn if you slept all day (I wish). It's an important baseline because your body needs at the very minimum this much to survive (note: this does not mean function properly).

TDEE

TDEE is your Total Daily Energy Expenditure. This is how much energy your body uses day to day, based on the level of activity you selected above. If you're maintaining your weight and are exercising regularly, you should aim to be eating this amount of calories per day.

Protein

Proteins are the building blocks of muscle (#gainz) and prevent muscle loss during a calorie deficit. Proteins should be your go to macronutrient because it helps to control appetite and makes you feel full for longer. Especially if you're vegan or vegetarian, it's important that you get enough protein in your diet from a variety of sources.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are a source of energy and are stored in the liver, brain, blood, and muscles as glycogen. Make sure you're mostly eating whole grains and not processed carbs. Complex carbs also take longer to digest, so you'll be feeling full for longer. Even though low-carb diets are popular, be aware they can cause intense cravings for donuts and ice cream when you're being too restrictive.

Fats

Fats have a bad rep, but they're an essential nutrient that our body needs to function. Fats help with hormone regulation, brain function, vitamin absorption, and so much more. It's important that you're consuming healthy fats such as in fish, nuts, and extra virgin olive oil while avoiding trans fats.

Time to Track!

We suggest using MyFitnessPal to track what you eat. Once you create an account, you can edit your goals with the numbers we calculated for you. MyFitnessPal tends to underestimate the number of calories a person needs, so make sure they match your TDEE!
